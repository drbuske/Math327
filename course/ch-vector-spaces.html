    Section Vector Space Axioms

    We now formulate the common structure you observed above. Throughout, let \(K\) be a field (e.g., \(\mathbb{R}\) or \(\mathbb{C}\)).
    ğŸ”—

    Definition 3. Vector space over a field.

    A vector space over \(K\) consists of a set \(V\) together with operations of addition \(V\times V\to V\text{,}\) \((u,v)\mapsto u+v\text{,}\) and scalar multiplication \(K\times V\to V\text{,}\) \((k,u)\mapsto ku\text{,}\) such that for all \(u,v,w\in V\) and \(k,\ell\in K\text{,}\) the following hold:
    ğŸ”—
    1. Commutativity: \(u+v=v+u\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    2. Associativity: \((u+v)+w=u+(v+w)\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    3. Additive identity: there is \(0\in V\) with \(u+0=u\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    4. Additive inverse: each \(u\) has \(-u\) with \(u+(-u)=0\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    5. Distributivity (vectors): \(k(u+v)=ku+kv\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    6. Distributivity (scalars): \((k+\ell)u=ku+\ell u\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    7. Associativity (scalars): \(k(\ell u)=(k\ell)u\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    8. Multiplicative identity: \(1u=u\text{.}\)
      ğŸ”—
    ğŸ”—
